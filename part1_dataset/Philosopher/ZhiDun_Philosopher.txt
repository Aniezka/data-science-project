Zhi Dun (Chinese: 支遁; pinyin: Zhī Dùn; Wade–Giles: Chih-tun; 314-366 CE) was a Chinese Buddhist monk and philosopher. A Chinese author, scholar, and confidant of Chinese government officials in 350 CE, he claimed that all who followed Buddhism would, at the end of their life, enter Nirvana.
