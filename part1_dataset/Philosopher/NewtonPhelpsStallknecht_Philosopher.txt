He was a president of the Metaphysical Society of America.  Stallknecht was educated at Princeton University, achieving his A.B. in 1927, A.M. in 1928, and Ph.D. in 1930.  During World War II, he was attached to the United States Army Security Agency in Washington.  His publications cover both philosophy and comparative literature, with a philosophical focus on Immanuel Kant, Henri Bergson, and Alfred North Whitehead.
