Michael A. Brown, CBE, DL, FInstP, FIET, FRSA, was the 2nd Vice-Chancellor of Liverpool John Moores University in Liverpool, England, United Kingdom between 2000 and 2011. He attended Bridgend Grammar School for Boys. In 1971, he obtained his PhD at the University of Nottingham with a thesis entitled Investigation of Phonon Scattering and Cross-relaxation by Paramagnetic Ions Using Thermal Conductivity Techniques. In May 2011, Michael Brown delivered the 100th lecture of the Liverpool John Moores University Roscoe Lectures at the Liverpool Philharmonic Hall, as named after by William Roscoe.
