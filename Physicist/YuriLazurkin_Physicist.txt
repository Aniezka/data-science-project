Yuri Semenovich Lazurkin (1916 - August 5, 2009) was a Russian physicist and a founder of a new discipline DNA physics. Lazurkin started his career in a novel field polymer physics. During World War II, he was in the Navy and worked on demagnetizing of ships. After the war, he returned to polymer physics. One of the phenomenons that his laboratory studied and greatly contributed to its understanding, was DNA melting.
