Valentine Louis Telegdi (Hungarian: Telegdi Bálint; 11 January 1922 – April 8, 2006) was a Hungarian-born American physicist. He was the Enrico Fermi Distinguished Service Professor of Physics at the University of Chicago before he moved to ETH Zürich. After retiring from ETH he divided his time between CERN and the California Institute of Technology. Telegdi chaired CERN's scientific policy committee from 1981 to 1983.[citation needed] According to György Marx he was one of The Martians. In 1991 he shared the Wolf Prize in Physics with Maurice Goldhaber.[citation needed]  He was elected a Foreign Member of the Royal Society (ForMemRS) in 2003.
