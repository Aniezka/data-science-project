Arun Kumar Choudhury (January 6, 1923 - September 6, 1987) was the founding head of the Department of Computer Science and Engineering at the University of Calcutta. He was a pioneer in both Analog and Digital Computing, since 1950's.  A.K. Choudhury Memorial Lectures are arranged at various Indian Universities to celebrate his contributions. Choudhury was instrumental in building the first analog computer in India. Each year, lectures on Computer Science are arranged in memory of Choudhury's achievements. The Information Technology College at Calcutta University is named after Choudhury.
